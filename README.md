<h1>Description</h1>
<p><strong>Algorithmic Advances for Single-Cell and Spatial Omics Data Analysis</strong><br>
With recent technological breakthroughs in single-cell and spatial transcriptomics, as well
as spatial proteomics and other spatially resolved omics platforms, researchers now have
access to molecular data at unprecedented resolution in both cellular identity and spatial
context. These technologies have opened new frontiers in understanding tissue
organization, developmental biology, and disease pathology. However, extracting
meaningful insights from these complex, high-dimensional, and often noisy datasets
requires the development of novel computational methods and algorithmic frameworks.
[...] (content unchanged for description)
</p>

<h2>Topics of Interest</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Computational methods for analyzing single-cell and spatial transcriptomics data</li>
  <li>Algorithms for spatial domain and tissue architecture identification</li>
  <li>Methods for inferring cell–cell communication and signaling networks</li>
  <li>Methods for exploring the tumor microenvironment</li>
  <li>Joint analysis of spatial transcriptomics, images, and spatial proteomics</li>
  <li>Machine learning and deep learning approaches for multi-omics integration</li>
  <li>Tools for trajectory inference and lineage tracing</li>
  <li>3D reconstruction and spatial modeling of tissues</li>
  <li>Denoising, imputation, deconvolution and normalization methods</li>
  <li>Batch correction, reference mapping, cross-sample alignment</li>
  <li>Applications in biology, neuroscience, immunology, cancer research</li>
</ul>
